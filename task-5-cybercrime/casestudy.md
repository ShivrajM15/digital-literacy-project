### UPI Payment Fraud – Case Study

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting college students in India. It typically occurs when attackers trick users into authorizing payments instead of receiving money.

In a common scenario, a student lists a product for sale online. A scammer contacts them pretending to be a buyer and agrees to pay via UPI. Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a “collect request” and asks the student to enter their UPI PIN to “receive” the payment. Trusting the message, the student enters their PIN, unknowingly authorizing a debit transaction.

In some cases, fraudsters also send fake payment screenshots or phishing links that mimic real apps like Google Pay or PhonePe. These links may steal login credentials or redirect users to malicious pages.

Students and young adults are frequently targeted because they actively use digital payment platforms and may not fully understand transaction flows.

The consequences include immediate financial loss, difficulty in recovering funds, and emotional stress. In many cases, victims realize the fraud only after the money has been deducted, making recovery harder.

This highlights the importance of understanding how digital payments work and recognizing common scam tactics.
